Part Two: The Psychological Health of the Camp Inmate
Episode 7: The Dehumanizing Camp Environment

This episode explores the harsh and dehumanizing conditions of concentration camps during World War II. Frankl vividly describes the physical and psychological torment endured by inmates. We delve into the profound suffering experienced by those subjected to forced labor, malnutrition, and the constant threat of death. Despite these extreme conditions, Frankl observes that individuals retained their inner freedom to choose their responses, highlighting the resilience of the human spirit.
