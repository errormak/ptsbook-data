Episode 29: Prospect Theory Revisited

In this episode, Kahneman revisits Prospect Theory and explores how it relates to real-world decisions. He discusses the implications of Prospect Theory for economic and policy-related choices.

Kahneman provides examples of how people's preferences for potential gains and losses can influence their behavior in various contexts, including healthcare decisions and investment strategies. He explains how framing choices differently can lead to different outcomes.

Understanding how Prospect Theory applies to everyday decisions highlights the importance of considering the psychological biases that affect our preferences for potential gains and losses. It underscores the need for policymakers and individuals to be aware of these biases when making choices.
