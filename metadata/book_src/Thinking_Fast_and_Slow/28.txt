Episode 27: Risk Policies

In this episode, Kahneman explores how individuals and organizations create risk policies based on their attitudes towards potential gains and losses. He discusses how these policies can significantly impact decision-making and risk management.

Kahneman provides examples of how different industries, such as finance and healthcare, develop risk policies that may deviate from traditional economic models. He explains how the framing of choices and policies can affect people's perceptions of risk and their willingness to accept it.

Understanding the role of risk policies in shaping decision-making is essential for recognizing how organizations and individuals navigate complex environments. It highlights the need for aligning risk policies with the specific attitudes and preferences of decision-makers.
